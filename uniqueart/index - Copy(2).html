Fulton Theater

The Fulton Opera House, also known as the Fulton Theatre or simply The Fulton, is a League of Regional Theatres class B regional theater located in historic downtown Lancaster, Pennsylvania. It is reportedly the oldest working theatre in the United States. It was designated a National Historic Landmark in 1964.

Grist Mill

Mascot Roller Mills, also known as Ressler's Mill, is a historic grist mill complex located at Upper Leacock Township, Lancaster County, Pennsylvania. The original section of the mill was built in 1737. The machinery was installed in 1906. It is the oldest continuously operating grist mill in Lancaster County.

Amish Plow

The Amish often use horses to plant and plow their fields instead of tractors and combines. Many Amish also avoid using pesticides. Instead, many Amish famers use manure to fertilize the land. They also use crop rotation to keep the soil healthy.

Buggy

The Amish buggy is a common sight along the country roads of Lancaster County. Although the Amish religion allows the Amish people to ride in automobiles, as well as buses and trains, they are forbidden to own one.
